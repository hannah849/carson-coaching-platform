Copy and CTAs should feel calm, grounded, intelligent, non-mystical, and non-salesy.

**Why:** Carson explicitly flagged this. The current draft was already an improvement but the direction should be reinforced in all future copy work.

**How to apply:**
- Write like a thoughtful essayist, not a conversion copywriter
- Avoid mystical language ("the universe", "higher self", "energy", "transformation container")
- Avoid salesy urgency ("step into", "unlock", "claim your")
- Prefer precise, specific, declarative sentences over evocative but vague ones
- CTA phrasing should be calm and editorial — e.g. "Read the Writing" not "Get Started", "Book a Session" not "Start Your Journey"
- Substack should feel like a natural first step before coaching — a trust builder and top-of-funnel entry point, not just a supplementary link
